The Problem

Surgeons operate for hours under fatigue.

Today's da Vinci robots are passive tools with zero autonomy.

Surgical errors rise sharply after long procedures.

Our Vision

We are building a surgical robotics Vision-Language-Action foundation model that learns from millions of surgical demonstrations to perform tasks like tissue handling, needle passing, and knot tying.

The first scalable, interpretable, and steerable surgical foundation model.

Our Approach

relative-motion policies
training directly on noisy surgical data without recalibration
spatial reasoning traces
making robot actions interpretable and editable by surgeons
steerable autonomy
letting surgeons guide and correct robots in real time
building with:
PyTorch • IsaacSim • ROS2 • CUDA • Transformer architectures
